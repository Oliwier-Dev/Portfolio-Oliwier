Mindset
How to change your mindset
Your mindset affects almost everything you do. It changes what you notice, what you try, and what you do when things get difficult.
There are lots of different tactics to change your mindset, but honestly, I like one that works the best for me. If you want to change your mindset around something, you need to delete the old one and then acquire the new one.
If you want to think differently, spend more time around better ideas. Read about them, listen to people who understand them, and test them yourself. First, what you want to do is to retrain your attention as much as possible on a topic that you believe in and trust, but that is limiting your potential. Such as “School is good,” on which I’ll touch in the next chapter.
It’s really simple, all you need to do is just listen over and over again, or do the same process over and over again until your mind accepts it. It will contradict the old thinking and allow the new “Mindset” to emerge.


Here I’ll give you some short examples on how I did it all.


A few days ago, I was actually a bit against coffee and thought that it might be bad for your health and stuff, which turned out to be an oversimplification. I looked at the evidence, considered how it affected me, and changed my mind. So I learned about it more and more, all the benefits it has on me, until my mind gave up and said, “You know what, coffee is good.” That’s when I started drinking coffee, and I felt more focused, although that feeling did not last forever. But I’ll talk about it in the next chapters at the end of this book.
The next example is me from a year or two ago, when I first started meditation. I was against it because I thought it was some satanic and demonic thing. But then I learned more and more that’s good and conditioned myself in this way. Later I found out that it might connect you closer to god, and all of the benefits of it, such as better memory, experiencing less stress, higher self-awareness, and better self-control.


Summary
If you want to change your mindset around something, retrain your attention by constantly learning more and more about the topic.

Productivity
You won’t succeed without this skill
Today's world moves so fast, our brains cannot keep up with it.

This is true because before, like in the past, you only needed to be talented at one or two things, and you could succeed. But now you need to be talented at at least 4 things to succeed in the internet and the way work happens now.
If you’re selling a course on something on you social medias, you need to know how to make content in which you need to know how to copywriting and make compelling and hooking content, you need to know how to film like professionals you need to know how to edit, and all the packaging and psychology that goes into that. Then you need to deliver on what you promise, make a course somehow. It might seem like everything is tied together because it is, but don’t let it fool you.
Because if you want to succeed with your course, you need to master or at least be quite good in all of those areas, not to mention you need to do it all fast, because while you might be learning a trending editing style, it might be dead after 2 months. It might be dead before you even master it and take advantage of it. And that is if you already know all of the marketing, psychology, copywriting, and script writing, and all the rest. But for a beginner in any specific niche or side hustle, It’s important to master many skills fast. And you might have heard the 10,000 hours rule. Which means that to master a skill, you need to practice it over 10,000 hours. But that is not true. If you commit, you can easily be very good at editing and content creation after just 1000 hours of editing. Which might sound a little compared to 10,000 hours, but remember it’s 1 / 8 of the entire film editing. It’s like spending 3 hours a day consistently every day for a year only doing content creation. After a year or 1000 hours, you will master or be very good at it.
Now it’s also impossible to master anything, because we are humans and we are made to make mistakes.


Even the best masters at karate, kung fu, football, tennis, swimming, or any sport, could try to maybe move their arm or foot 0.3 cm longer away from their bodies. Even if someone says that they mastered something, they didn’t because you can always improve something, even by 0.1 mm.


Not that we know that it’s theoretically impossible to master anything, let's talk again about how wrong the 10,000 rule is. You can learn a specific skill or parkour move under 5 hours, which is almost done at top performance.
You can “master” the push-up, squat from in, maybe 10 hours. Of doing it.

Simple!

The best time management hacks
If you have little time each day, this will be a golden chapter for you.
You see, there are a lot of different time management techniques, but most apply to just 5% of the population. They are irrelevant to most people’s situation because if you come late from school, then you can’t do much at home, which will save lots of time for you.
But some techniques will still help you get more time without draining you or being too complex to do.


You see the difference between the techniques that I will say here and the ones that other people tell you.
The ones that I will tell you are simple, but many people overlook them because they are so simple. Many people think that if something is simple, it’s not working or it’s simply outdated and overused.
But that’s simply not true, because even though over 2 billion know what meditation is, it still works, and you will be more mindful. The same applies to the techniques I’m about to tell you, that you might not be doing.




1. Delete tasks


Many people think that in order to be more productive and find more time, you need to add tasks, like reading, organizing your workspace, or something.
But that’s not true because most people are doing too many things, and that is stopping them from achieving success. Instead, what you want to do is to find the 5 most time-consuming tasks that don’t bring much value to your life. Like school, too-long skincare routine, or the time it takes to travel from place A, like your house, to place B.


Now, when you analyze those exact habits or tasks, you can start either deleting them or minimizing them.
Now you probably wonder how you’re supposed to delete or minimize school, but it’s much simpler than you think, because during breaks you can journal, meditate, or read. If you travel by bus, you can work there on your phone. If you’re traveling in a car, you can listen to a self-help podcast because even if you don’t remember what was said on it, your subconscious mind will. That will make you subconsciously more conscious of that information. If you learn that information later in life, it will be much easier for you to apply it.
So, try to find as many time-drainers as possible each week or month and try to eliminate them.


Now, the second technique is to work on your own business or side hustles in school or at work. For example, if you’re done with a specific work task, instead of resting, you could meditate for 10 minutes and reset, then work again but on your own stuff.


The same applies to school, if you’re on a low-value lesson, you can work on it or try to skip it somehow in a way that you don’t get caught, so you can work then.




2. Have a routine


Now this might feel like something simple, and you know it, but are you doing it?
All of the successful people have or had a specific routine, and it’s one of the simplest building blocks to build success that is overlooked.
They work so well because a routine helps you minimize all the friction between getting to work and working.
It’s what I’m doing for a month right now, and it helps me more than anything else.


You should have a routine for waking up and getting ready to attack the day, you should have a routine to get ready to start working, no matter the time of the day, you should have a sleep routine.


For example, my wake-up routine is really simple yet effective: 20 push-ups, 20 squats, write down all my goals that I set the day before on paper, and then immediately start working for at least 30 min. Then Itake a cold shower. This routine makes me ready for the day. This routine boosts my alertness, by natural dopamine, and wakes me up.


Now you probably think that I shared almost nothing in this chapter, but this is enough, combined with the tracker I said in the previous chapter, to get more time and become much more productive.


Summary
If you want to find more time:
Find 5 worthless tasks or thing you do or go to and eliminate or minimize them
Have a routine
Use my tracking technique that was covered in the previous chapter.


































































The best mental energy savers
No matter how much time you have, if you can’t master this chapter's information, you won't succeed. There is no point in listening to podcasts or videos on focus and time management if you’re constantly mentally tired. And most people don’t realize it because their self-awareness is very weak (Which I will be talking about how to boost and improve in the next chapters).


You see, your problem isn’t that you’re lazy or unproductive; you’re simply mentally drained. I had the same problem where I was thinking that I’m so lazy and unproductive, I will never get this done. But I was just telling myself very bad affirmations that were doing even more harm than good (I’ll talk about it in the next chapters).
The same applies to 99.5% of people on the earth, they don’t realize what I realized at 14 until they are 65 years old.


So to improve your mental energy, all you have to do is simply reduce distractions and minimize almost everything you do in life. By saying this, I don’t mean to minimize everything like shortening your prayers, sleep time, and so on. Instead, I mean to organize and sell different items in your room. Don’t talk so often to your friends, of course still talk, but just a bit less. It will make you more mysterious, and they will often like you more while saving a lot of mental energy.
Have a routine, as I said in the previous chapter, because then your brain follows specific actions.
“The hardest isn’t doing it, it’s starting it.”

Even if you know how to do something and you’re about to do it, the start will be the hardest and most unproductive part of the whole block if you don’t have a routine.
For example, if you’re meditating, reading, journaling, exercising, and stretching every day, and you're doing that at different times then try to follow it all into a specific routine. Same time same thing.


Try to do less crazy things during the day, for example, if you like to sometimes have lots of fun and go a bit crazy, then you should minimize it. Of course, if you want to have more mental energy.
Now it’s good to have sometimes   fun and go completely crazy, but don’t do it often. Do it on days that you have planned fewer goals and first do them and later have fun, with friends or whatever. Twice a week, maximum.




But now, for all of those who are in school or work, the techniques that I listed will still work, but not as well. Because when you go to school or work, it drains most of your mental energy that you have for the day.
There are some techniques to do that, some that I use daily and highly recommend, and some that I  still use, but don’t recommend at all.


First, the ones that I recommend.
Try to have a very stable sleep routine and sleep times. For example, if you go to school/work at 8 am, then try to go to sleep around 10 pm, then wake up around 6 am.
While doing this, have a 5-minute short routine to wake up, like drinking a cup of water and doing a few push-ups or squats. Then work for 30 min or 1h, as much as you can. Then get ready for school/work and try to minimize all different and possible mental energy drainers, like scrolling on the phone and constantly talking to other people.


At home, when you come from school/work, try to have a meal, and just sit somewhere or lie down (not in bed) and just close your eyes, and meditate for 10 min. This will make the waves in your brain more rhythmic, which naturally improves your focus and well-being.
If you still feel tired after it all, consider going on a walk, ideally in nature, because it improves so many things. Not only will you get fresher oxygen into your lungs, but you will also be more productive because of all of the psychological improvements nature gives you.




Now I will tell you the ones that I also use, but don’t recommend for a typical person. It will mostly apply if you go to school, and that is to skip some lessons. If you have an old teacher who doesn’t recognize everything, what happens around him. Then use it to your advantage. Try to go in the first 5 minutes of the lesson and then say you’re going to the toilet, or try to go sneakily so that he doesn’t see you.
Then, if it’s your last lesson, go home, or simply work.


I don’t recommend it because it might get you in trouble, but I’m just me, and I do much more extreme things to get more time and save up mental energy for the work blocks.




Summary
You’re not lazy, your mental energy is drained. Try to minimize events in your life and have many different routines for different tasks. Work before school/work, minimize tasks that drain a lot of your mental energy at school or work. Meditate, walk in nature.
































































The best focus management hacks
This chapter is for someone who has already applied the last two pieces of information. Everything in the time management chapter and the mental energy saving one.
If you still didn’t apply what I said there, this chapter won’t help you much.
You see, I made this part of the book in order. First, you need to have more time, then energy, and then finally focus.
If you want the greatest improvement from this part, do as I suggest.
For those who read and applied the lessons in the last two chapters, congrats, and here are the best focus management hacks for you that I use daily.


1. Routine
I already covered it many times. But basically, what it does is reduce friction between personal to professional life.


2. Track hours
If you’re using Windows 11 to work, always track how many hours you work and note them in your track. You can use the “Clock app” on Windows 11.
If you don’t have Windows 11 or 10, use your phone or download another app that tracks time. This motivates you to achieve a specific time spent on a project on a daily basis.


3. Do light exercise
Always before you start working, even if you’re already productive, try to do 10 or 20 push-ups and squats. This makes your blood circulation better and improves everything, from your ability to concentrate to your overall performance.
This is the one focus management hack that helped me the most.


4. Friend
If you've got a friend with whom you’re talking about your goals and stuff. Consider having a call with him to work together, even if it’s digital and you’re working on different projects.


5. Drink lots of water
Consider doing a water bulk and drink a little over 2L of water daily, no matter if you’re 14 or 30.


6. Work in intervals
Use the Pomodoro technique, where you work for 25 minutes, then rest for 5 minutes, and then work again, or do as I do. Depending on whether your task is hard or easy, add a few minutes of work or rest. Instead of 25 minutes of work, you can do 60 minutes of work and 10 minutes of rest.

Forget traditional goal setting, do this instead
Almost every person who sets goals does it wrong. many people who set goals fail with them year in and year out. And that’s often because of their goal-setting plans. Most people often set goals that are way too unrealistic to achieve in a day or year.
Most people overestimate their chances of success in the short term but underestimate their chances of success in the long term, like 10 years. Not to mention that there are lots of gurus that are selling productivity courses, on their social media, they are telling you to set high goals, only so that you fail with them and buy their course.
The problem is even deeper than that because when you set such goals, almost in every situation, you will blame yourself. If you don't achieve them, you will blame yourself, and in the end, it all makes you feel much worse and think that you’re lazy or unproductive.
That reflects deeply on your self-worth and transfers to other areas of your life.


To avoid this, you should be using my goal-setting process.
First, there are two different ways of doing it, and it depends on who you are currently. If you’re just starting with entrepreneurship and don’t have a clear goal in life, and don’t know yet what you want to do in your future life, then use this way of setting your goals.
First, set a goal you want to achieve in a year. Ideally, at the beginning of the year, or a goal that needs to be done and accomplished before the end of the year. You could use the SMART goal framework, but I would say that it’s a bit bad and not working very well. Even if you hit all of those criteria in the SMART framework, you might still set it wrong. Because you might have set it in a result-based way, more specifically, a way where you want to achieve a specific result after it.
For example, these are the result-oriented goals.
* Get 1K subs on YouTube
* Be more mindful
* Get in great shape.


Now, if you analyze this, how are you supposed to know what you need to do in order to hit 1K subs?
How do you track if you’re more mindful or if you achieved the goal you wanted?
What does “Great shape” mean? For one, it might mean losing 15 kg of weight. And for another person, it might mean to have 10% body fat.
As you see, the main problem with all of those goals is that you don’t know exactly what to do to achieve this goal.


They feel and seem good, because everyone would rather see the result that is waiting for you, but they are a trap. The actionable goals are less attractive because you don’t see the main result of them, but it’s better for productivity.
These are examples of actionable goals
* Meditate for 3000 minutes in a year
* Post 52 YouTube videos
* Have 100 gym sessions
Now, the thing behind those goals is that you don’t know exactly what the result of those goals might be, because if you’re posting 52 really bad videos, the probability of getting 1k subs is low. But if you try to improve on each video and try your best, the possibility of getting to 1k subs is high.
The same applies to all other actionable goals. Try your best and you will succeed.


Now, after you have set your yearly goal, you need to break it down into a monthly goal. Use the same technique as you used in the yearly goal setting.
If you wonder why you should break them down into smaller goals, that is because they are easier to achieve, and you can act now to achieve them.
They should also be actionable as before. I always recommend focusing on 4 to 2 goals, so you can add one more goal here, such as “Practice X, Y times”. Just note that this is just an example, and your goal could be completely different.


Do the same for weeks, but here you can add some goals that don’t directly align with your main ones, but still need to be achieved. They should be the smaller, broken-down goals of the monthly ones.


Do the same for daily goals, actionable, and based on the weekly goals. Here, your goals should be much easier to do than the weekly ones.
“Script the video”, “Practice X,” and so on.
Then there are a few more important steps to take. Such as writing under the goals the date or time they need to be achieved, for example, 12 - 14 edit. (for the daily goal.)
Now do the same for weekly and monthly goals, but not directly on the yearly ones, because they are quite hard to predict, and you might change direction during the year.
One more thing is to write them in your note app, and make a screenshot, then put it in your wallpaper to remember them daily. (Do this only with daily goals.)
One last thing to do is to have a to-do list. Where they don’t have any timeframe. They should be just notes on what to do in life. So, for example, they could be such as. “Copy the whole system into X,” something that isn’t urgent but still should be done.




Now, as I said, there are two different ways, and now I’ll tell you the second one. It’s the same, but now you also add a 5-year goal you want to achieve, ideally one or two. With this “5-year goal,” you don’t renew it every 5 years. Instead, you renew it every single year.
It might seem weird, but it’s much better this way.
By doing this, you can adjust your main goal and overall every goal based on what you want to do. So, for example, if it’s the middle of the year and you want to be a professional mountain biker, then your goals won't be relevant to you. If you were following your plan and renewing it every 5 years. Then it would do more harm than good.
Now you probably wonder why you should do this.
By doing this, you will have a clearer picture of what your yearly, monthly, weekly, and daily goals should look like. But I only recommend this if you already have something running, like a personal brand, a course, or anything that makes you some money and can be easily scaled.

Motion creates motion (Part 1)
You may or may not be familiar with the concept in physics, where if an object is in motion, it’s easier to keep it in motion. This is because of Inertia.
When an object stays at rest, it’s harder to move it because of “static inertia”.
While if an object is in motion, you only deal with friction if there is any, but it’s still weaker compared to the “static inertia”.
Which is why it’s easier to keep in motion.


The same law applies to life and productivity. If you start your day with motion, such as not using your phone in the morning, taking a cold shower, having light exercise for 5 minutes, or anything that’s a win, that will compound and make the rest of the day more productive.
How?
Because you overcame the “static inertia”, which is the hardest. It’s hard to start with anything, like imagine yourself waking up at 6 am, then working 2 hours straight. Sounds impossible, right?
But if you have any slight motion in the few minutes of the day, it will become easy.
You see, most people are most productive in the morning of the day, because they have the most energy and mental will.
But most people waste it scrolling or starting the day in the wrong way. But here is the catch: if you, for example, wake up at 6 am, as I said, and you set up your alarm 3 meters away from you, so that you need to physically get up to turn it off. Then you turn on all the lights, while having little movement in your body, so that your blood circulates better. Then, after that, try to make your bed, do light exercise (even for 1 min) like a few squats or push-ups. And then try to work.
Set a specific goal for how long you want to work, and do it.


And now not only have you just created a morning routine, and also applied the “motion creates motion” principle. You woke up and started the day with smaller wins to overcome the “static inertia”.
Now you have overcome the problem with waking up and immediately working, but you didn’t overcome one thing.
To keep the momentum…








You see, in physics, there is a thing in Newton's first law, which we talked about, that “an object in motion will only slow down if there is friction.”
Now, in a perfect world, there is no friction, but our world is not perfect.


That’s why you need to have a routine, routines directly minimize the friction between work and not working.
Because you don’t need to use your mental energy to decide what you do (Which is friction). As I said, the morning routine I gave you has almost 0% friction between waking up and work, because it starts small and then gets bigger and bigger.


It’s exactly what every other routine should do. Start small, then go bigger.
My biggest challenge in productivity isn’t to stay productive, but to be productive after any event, after breakfast.
I often like to have even 5 minutes of work after breakfast and before an event, because that creates motion.
For example, today, I’m going to church directly after breakfast, so what I’ll do is open my Google Doc, write the first sentence in a chapter, and then get ready to go to church. This way, I not only created motion, but I also set a priority in my mind that I need to be done with X after church.


Summary
Motion creates motion.
Have motion at the beginning of the day, and create routines that reduce friction.

Cognitive Biases
This is an important chapter if you want to learn how our brain works and what mistakes it makes. So that you don’t repeat the same mistake and use them to your advantage.
1. Sunk cost fallacy
It’s a classical one, but one that’s also very destructive.
I’ll give an immediate example of how I experienced its effects yesterday, when I ended 8th grade.


I went out with friends to just say goodbye for 2 months, and went to a restaurant. I ordered two pizzas, but I didn’t think they would be so big, so I ate the first one, and then when the second one arrived, I was already full. I ate half of the second pizza and then felt guilty and a bit frustrated that Ithrew away money. But no matter if I ate the pizza or not, the money was already given to the restaurant.
Which means that I cannot do anything to make the actual situation better. When Itried to eat the whole pizza, I felt too full, and it would be much wiser to throw it, or take it home (Which I didn’t consider, because of the sunk cost fallacy).
After I had eaten the pizza and headed home, I realized that I had forgotten this bias. If I remembered it, not only would I make a smarter decision, but I would also feel better.
Now this is on a small scale, but there were times when people went crazy because of this thinking problem.


2. Confirmation bias
 Every bias is dangerous, and the same follows for this one. What it does is simple, you only agree with information that you already believe in.
For example, if you believe that all rich people are greedy, and then you see 10 rich people in front of you, 9 are kind but one is rude, and because of this bias, you will say, “See? I knew it, all the rich are greedy.” That happens just because you believe in something wrong. Even worse, you completely ignore the fact that 9 were kind, because what you believe is stronger than the truth.


That’s also why a story is always stronger than the truth when it comes to marketing.


It’s dangerous because without seeing its effects, you will never grow or change your beliefs; you just keep repeating the same ones.
3. Bandwagon bias
You do something just because everyone else is doing it.
For example, if all of your friends are buying a course, then you will think that it’s very valuable and you need to buy it too, even if you don’t know what it is.


Here, the emotional thinking is at play, and because it’s not as accurate as the conscious thinking, you may buy a scammy course.
Be aware of something that is too hyped, manipulators love this, so they create something hyped so people can join in without thinking.


An example from the real world, I don’t know if you know him or not, but there is a person called Iman Gadzhi (2025). Many people follow him just because he uses these false thinking problems.
He created a huge personal brand, and people follow the movement according to his bias. So that’s how he became rich. What he did was create a movement and add a lot of scarcity to it. As I said before, fear and greed are the most manipulative emotions in us. He uses both in unethical ways.
Be aware of such people and avoid them as much as you can.
If you see a book that has sold 4 million copies, it doesn’t mean it provides good value; all it means is that people like it, and you can do that by using a lot of emotional manipulation in your sentences.


4. Authority Bias
You believe in someone just because they are seen as an “expert” or have power or status.
It’s exactly what Iman Gadzhi uses, and many other fake course sellers.
If you see a guy in a suit with 1M followers and he says,
“This $1000 course will make you rich”. You believe him because he looks successful, but he might not even have made all of this money from the thing he is teaching you. The real truth is that 95% of course sellers got their money from the course and not from what they teach. If they earned more from the thing they teach, they would not make a course in the first place. And yet there are millions of courses in this world. Why?


Because a course is much easier to sell and make money from than actually doing a thing that requires a lot of effort.
The best courses are either the ones from people who have been in the game for a long time or know what they’re talking about. So it’s important to use your different types of thinking to analyze, did this person make this money from the course of the thing he sells?


Fake gurus use this bias to look powerful and gain your trust, even if their advice is worthless.


5. Anchoring bias
Your brain sticks to the first number or info it sees, even if it’s random.
So, for example, if a course is “normally” priced at 5000$ but now it’s 997$, your brain will think Wow, this is a huge discount, because it’s anchored on the 5000$.
If it’s also combined with a limited offer, it’s even more powerful, and the sales will be exponential.
But in reality, the course might never have been even priced at 5000$; this is a very dangerous marketing tactic to watch out for, because you think you’re getting the deal, but it’s a simple marketing trick.

Authority

Framing & Reframing
“It’s not what you say, but it’s how you frame it.”

There are a few strategies to frame something correctly.
1. Anchoring
Instead of giving one fixed price, like 49$ for a product or service you sell.
Include a small note that you got a discount, from 149$ to 49$. This makes the 49$ seem small to the 149$, even if it never was priced at 149$.
If you use scarcity, it should be real. Do not invent a deadline just to pressure someone.


2. Contrast
Have two options to make one seem like a better deal.
Instead of having one offer, make a second one. It doesn’t need to be better.
If it’s even worse than your main offer, then that’s good.
Make the worst offer seem overpriced to make the main one feel better, like the logical offer. (Even if it’s also a bit overpriced.)
But you can also include three offers.
The first one, which is the worst one, has a small price and minimal value, but still something that would be valuable to some people.
Then the middle offer needs to be overpriced, to make the most expensive one seem the best.
Because many people don’t want to overpay, but they also don’t want to have the worst possible alternative, so by doing this, you increase the chances of someone buying your bigger offer.


3. Loss aversion
Highlight what a person will lose if they don’t buy your offer. We tend to act when there is something we can lose. Because we hate to lose, that’s why loss aversion is a powerful and manipulative tactic.
Now, as you see, I explain and give you a lot of what to do.
But you also need to understand what not to do, because sometimes not knowing what not to do, hurts more than knowing what to do.




First, never try to use logic to get a person into an agreement. Remember, if someone hasn’t made a choice yet, use emotions.
People decide with emotions, and justify with logic.


Second, never argue over something if a person doesn’t want something, no matter how hard you try. Let go. Maybe they will come back later, but if you argue, they will lose all of your trust. And even worse, you will lose your reputation if this becomes something viral.


Third, never ignore anyone's current worldview or belief. Try to first understand what they believe in by analyzing deeply, or asking questions.
Always ask a lot of questions.


Last, never overload anyone with facts before they’re emotionally invested.
People decide with emotions, and justify with logic.
